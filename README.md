# kiro_spark_challenge

## Features

**Lattice** turns your course materials into an interactive visual map you can actually learn from — built specifically for students with ADHD and dyslexia.

- **Multi-source ingestion** — drop in a PDF, a YouTube link, an image of your notes, or an audio file. Lattice processes all of them together and pulls out the key ideas from each one.
- **Interactive concept map** — your material becomes a visual graph of connected ideas. Click or tap any concept to see its full definition, a plain-language explanation, and how it connects to everything else.
- **Every idea is source-backed** — Lattice only shows a concept if it can point to exactly where it came from. One tap jumps to the original page, video timestamp, or image region. Nothing is invented.
- **Contradiction surfacing** — when two sources disagree, Lattice shows both sides with their citations rather than picking one. You decide what to believe.
- **Depth layers** — explore ideas at overview, standard, or deep level. The map guides you from the big picture before diving into detail.
- **Real-time progress** — a live status feed tells you exactly which stage of processing is running, so you always know the graph is on its way.
- **ADHD mode** — dim everything except the concept you're on, pin a working memory list of facts you've established, get a one-tap recap of your last three interactions, and pick up exactly where you left off when you reopen the app. No streaks, no timers, no pressure.
- **Dyslexia mode** — choose between Lexend and OpenDyslexic fonts, enable sentence-per-line layout, follow along with a reading ruler, and use synchronized text-to-speech with word-level highlighting. Every text field also accepts voice input.
- **Switchable live** — flip between ADHD mode, dyslexia mode, or both at any time without losing your place. Both sets of supports layer cleanly together.
